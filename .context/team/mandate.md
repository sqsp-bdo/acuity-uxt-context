# UX Transformation Team — Mandate

## Mission

Redesign the end-to-end experience from first touch (ad/search/social) through first booking so that Acuity feels intuitive, guided, and "for businesses like mine" — for both appointment-based and class-based sellers.

## Primary Outcome

Increase the share of new trials that become **"open for business"** (have at least one bookable offering live and receive a first booking) within the trial window. Measured separately for appointment and class modalities.

## Scope

- **Marketing surfaces**: paid / organic / social and frontsite messaging
- **Product surfaces**: registration, trial creation, and setup to first booking on **mobile web** (primary), desktop, and admin app (third priority)

## Guiding Principles

1. **Optimize for clarity, guidance, and speed to value** — not configurability
2. **Modality-specific and mobile-web-first** — not one-size-fits-all or desktop-first
3. **Momentum beats completeness** — collect only what is essential up front; defer the rest until after the user has seen value
4. **The first booking is the "aha" moment — get users there fast**, even if imperfectly

## Hypotheses Under Test

1. **The first booking is the aha moment** — Prospects feel Acuity is real when they experience a booked appointment (even self-booked). Getting there fast matters more than getting there perfectly.

2. **Vocabulary vs. mental models** — "Appointment types," "availability," "calendars" don't match how sellers describe their work. We clash with real operating models.

3. **Not opinionated enough in setup** — We force users to design their own system instead of offering clear, opinionated starting points by modality and vertical.

4. **Haven't defined minimum viable inputs to go live** — We ask for too much before users see value. We over-optimize for completeness over momentum.

5. **Configurability over simplicity** — Years of additive iteration exposed complexity up front rather than progressively. Guided by default is the target state.

## How We Work

- **Cadence**: Strict two-week sprints. Every sprint must result in something testable — a prototype, experiment, or shipped change.
- **Weekly review covers**: Next problem area (PM, Marketing) / Design in progress (PM, PD, Marketing) / Sprint delivery (PM, Eng) / Learnings from prior delivery (PM, PA)
- **Measurement**: What we ship, what we learn, impact on metrics. Not artifacts produced.
- **Bias**: AI-enhanced ideation, design, and delivery. Bold, opinionated solutions. Incremental optimization is not transformation.
- **Constraint handling**: Ignore constraints in favor of ideal user experience, *then* figure out how to remove them. When opportunities warrant larger investment, defend with evidence.

## Department-Specific Prompts

**Marketing**
- "How do we signal, from the first impression, that Acuity is built for businesses like Mud Hut / Shannon Lee Esthetics / BK Metalworks?"
- "Where do our acquisition promises diverge from what the product actually does in the first 5 minutes?"

**Product**
- "What is our official definition of 'open for business' by modality, and how will we instrument it?"
- "What existing flows or requirements can we remove or push later without harming long-term success?"

**Design**
- "What does 'guided by default' look like not just in onboarding, but as a system that continues after first booking?"
- "How should appointment vs class journeys diverge visually and structurally so they reflect real operating models?"

**Research**
- "What are the 3–4 core questions we must answer about expectations, aha moments, and friction in trial to design the right paths?"

**PA / Data**
- "How do we build a standard, enduring view of the Interested → Open for Business funnel, by modality, device, and channel, that every team can see weekly?"
- "How might we validate UX transformation changes outside of A/B testing?" — For things we don't A/B test, holdout groups may be preferable to pre/post analysis given lower operational lift and fewer confounds.
